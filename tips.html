Top 5 Travel Tips

1. Get an Oyster Card or Use Contactless

Cash payments for transit are costly and inconvenient. For the cheapest tickets on the Tube, buses, and the majority of rail services, use a contactless card or device or purchase a Visitor Oyster card. It's the most economical mode of transportation.

2. Stand on the Right

Always stand on the right side of escalators on the London Underground so that passengers in a hurry can ascend on the left. Londoners take this unspoken rule very seriously!

3. Explore Beyond Zone 1

There is a lot more to see in the outer zones of London, even if central London (Zone 1) includes several well-known attractions. To get a different view of the city, go to places like Richmond, Hampstead Heath, or Greenwich.

4. Check for Free Museums

The Tate Modern, the National Gallery, and the British Museum are just a few of London's top museums and galleries that provide free admission to their permanent collections. Tickets for special exhibitions are often paid for.

5. Tipping is Not Always Necessary

A 12.5% service charge is frequently automatically added to the bill at restaurants. Tipping service is not standard. It's courteous to round up the fee for cabs.
